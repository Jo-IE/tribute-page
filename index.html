Eartha Mae Kitt

Singer, Actress, Legend

Here's a timeline of Eartha Kitt's wonderful life:

  • 1927: Born in South Carolina.
  • 1943: Kitt won a scholarship to study with Katherine Dunham, and later joined Dunham's dance troupe.
  • 1952: Kitt became a rising star with her appearance in the Broadway review New Faces of 1952
  • 1954: Kitt releases her first album
  • 1960s: Kitt played one of her most famous parts—the villainous vixen "Catwoman."
  • 1968: She criticized the Vietnam War during a luncheon with Lady Bird Johnson.
  • 1978: She earned a Tony Award nomination for her role in​ Timbuktu!
  • 2000: SKitt netted a Tony Award nomination for her work in The Wild Party with Toni Collette.
  • 2008: She passes away from colon cancer, aged 81.

"I am learning all the time. The tombstone will be my diploma."

- Eartha Kitt
